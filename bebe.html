Bebe = Drinks

Challenge 1:

Translate:
  • When is the time for a drink?
  • Whenever you are thirsty!
Dictionary:
  • ut = when
  • beba = drink
  • oc ut = whenever
  • siro = thirsty
  • tepa = time
Maj:
  • Ut sonu la tepa fo una beba?
  • Oc ut tu sonu siro!

Challenge 2:

Translate:
  • What can a man drink at a party?
  • Usually I drink a beer but sometimes wine!
Dictionary:
  • ke posu = what can
  • un xoma = a man
  • sh bebu = to drink
  • at una feta = at a party
  • bo algo-tepa = but sometimes
  • vino = wine
Maj:
  • Ke posu un xoma sh bebu at una feta?
  • Iujo ea bebu una bera bo algo-tepa vino!
Note: In some languages like Romanian and Russian "da" means "yes". In Maj, "bo" is using the meaning of "but". This is a conjunction. A preposition that connects arguments in a logical way.

Challenge 3:

Translate:
  • How many beers can a man drink before he is drunk?
  • Just a single beer of course!
Dictionary:
  • vi fil = how many
  • ate = before
  • ivro = drunk
  • ena = a (one)
  • solo = single
  • zogo = for sure / of course
Maj:
  • Vi fil bere posu un xoma sh bebu ate sonu ivro?
  • Djo ena solo bera bo zogo!

Challenge 4:

Translate:
  • What can a boy drink at his birthday party?
  • We serve to our children only water, milk or natural juice!
Dictionary:
  • noi = we
  • nose = ours
  • gatsa = boy
  • akva = water
  • leca = milk
  • nato = natural
  • soka = juice
  • kide = children
  • djo = only
Maj:
  • Ke posu un gatsa sh bebu la sua naco-feta?
  • Noi sevu zu nose kide djo akva, leca od nato-suka!

    • Note: In this example I have used pattern O-A to make a composite word: naco-feta = "birthday party" equivalent to expression: "feta de nacu". Both can be used having the same meaning.

    Challenge 5:

    Translate:
    • Who can drink alcoholic beverages?
    • Only healthy adults, which have nothing better to do!
    Dictionary:
    • ki = who
    • buja = booz (drink)
    • sano = healthy
    • dute = adults
    • xaru = to have
    • vexa = nothing
    • beta = better
    • sh faru = to do
    Maj:
    • Ki poso sh bebu buja?
    • Djo dute sano, uk xaru nika beta sh faru!

    Challenge 6:

    Translate:
    • What is your favorite drink?
    • The best drink is sparkling water!
    Dictionary:
    • uk = which
    • beta = best
    • miro = mineral
    • akva = water
    Maj:
    • Uk sonu al ti fevo beba?
    • Lo beta beba sonu miro-akva!

    Challenge 7:

    Translate:
    • Hello girl how are you?
    • Do you have money?
    Dictionary:
    • tcao = hello
    • Tcika = girl
    • faru = to do
    • gete = money
    • tenu = to hold
    Maj:
    • Tcao Tcika ke faru? = Hello girl how are you?
    • Bine, tenu gete? = Okay, do you have money?
    • No lo tenu = I don't have.
    • Poi, vale! = Then good bye!
